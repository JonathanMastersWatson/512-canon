### What Was Authored

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: 512-KERNEL · CVS-SIDECAR

#### 1. 512 Execution Constraint Kernel

The 512 Execution Constraint Kernel is a discovered constraint — seven
immutable invariants defining the minimum conditions under which execution
at machine speed can be considered legitimate.

The constraint set is non-ownable by design. It was discovered, not
invented. Physics and scale force it into existence regardless of whether
any specific documentation exists.

The written documentation describing the kernel is original authored work,
released under CC BY 4.0.

#### 2. Cryptographic Verification Sidecar (CVS)

The Cryptographic Verification Sidecar is an invented witness architecture.
It sits alongside a running system — never inside it — and records
cryptographic evidence of what occurred, when it occurred, and under what
declared constraints.

CVS is original engineering work, released under Apache License 2.0.

#### 3. c to ubiquity

Original book by Jonathan M. Watson. Full title: *c to ubiquity*.
Covers the physics of machine-speed governance, the 512 constraint kernel,
the CVS architecture, economic implications, institutional adoption paths,
and philosophical foundations.

Released under CC BY 4.0.
