### Abstract

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: 512-KERNEL · EXECUTION-BOUNDARY

AI inference is becoming a commodity traded at machine
speed. The infrastructure required to make inference
markets function — proof of delivery, atomic settlement,
execution-time verification — does not yet exist at scale.

This paper argues that 512 and CVS provide the missing
infrastructure layer. Without execution-time constraint
verification, inference markets cannot price risk,
cannot resolve disputes, and cannot satisfy enterprise
procurement requirements. With it, inference becomes
a fully tradeable commodity with verifiable delivery,
atomic settlement, and cryptographic proof of service.

### The Inference Market Problem

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: EXECUTION-BOUNDARY · TEMPORAL-INVERSION

Current AI billing operates on assertion. A request is
made. Compute is consumed. A bill is generated. The bill
asserts that compute was delivered at a certain level
of quality and quantity.

This creates three structural problems:

**No proof of execution** — the bill is self-reported
by the system being billed. There is no independent
verification that the declared compute was actually
consumed, under declared constraints, at declared
service levels.

**Disputes are interpretive** — when a buyer disputes
a bill, resolution requires human review of logs
generated by the system under dispute. The logs are
mutable. The system is the witness to its own billing.

**Cannot support autonomous agents** — AI agents
executing thousands of inference requests per second
cannot wait for monthly billing cycles or human
dispute resolution. Atomic settlement at execution
time is required.

### What Price Discovery Requires

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: 512-KERNEL · CVS-SIDECAR

For inference markets to function, price must emerge
from verified execution, not asserted delivery.

Three properties are required:

**Proof of delivery** — the buyer must be able to verify
cryptographically that the declared compute was executed
under declared constraints. Not claimed. Proven.

**Atomic settlement** — payment and proof of delivery
must settle together at execution time. Pre-payment
without proof creates fraud exposure. Post-payment
without proof creates dispute exposure.

**Deterministic pricing** — the cost of a given inference
request must be computable from observable execution
parameters, not estimated from historical averages or
subject to vendor discretion.

512 provides the constraint layer. CVS provides the
proof layer. Together they make all three properties
achievable.

### The Billable Unit

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: BILLABLE-UNIT-FORMULA

The billable unit for inference is a deterministic
function of observable execution parameters:
```
Billable_Units = depth_multiplier × Σ(similarity_score_i × token_weight_i)

depth_multiplier = 1 + (tokens_consumed ÷ context_budget_declared)
```

This formula produces a number that reflects actual
cognitive contribution — how deeply the retrieved
context influenced the output — rather than a flat
per-token rate that ignores relevance and depth.

The formula is:
- Deterministic: same inputs always produce same output
- Auditable: any party with the execution record can
  verify the calculation
- Reproducible: no vendor discretion in the calculation
- Aligned: deeper, more relevant usage earns more

### Market Structure

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: 512-KERNEL · EXECUTION-BOUNDARY

A 512-governed inference market operates as a spot
market for verified compute. Buyers declare intent.
Sellers declare constraints and pricing. The execution
boundary evaluates compatibility. If compatible,
execution proceeds and CVS records the delivery.
Settlement is atomic.

This structure enables:
- Competitive pricing across inference providers
- Verifiable service level agreements
- Automated dispute resolution by proof
- Insurance of inference delivery risk
- Composable inference pipelines with per-step billing

None of these are possible without execution-time
proof of delivery. All of them are possible with it.

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: 512-KERNEL

AI model capability is commoditizing rapidly. Training
costs fall 50% annually. Frontier capability is
available to anyone with an API key.

The durable economic position in the AI compute economy
belongs to whoever controls the execution boundary —
where liability terminates, compliance is provable,
and enterprise contracts are signed.

> Those are not the same place."

The inference market layer built on 512 and CVS is
not a product. It is infrastructure. Infrastructure
compounds in value as the intelligence layer above
it commoditizes.
