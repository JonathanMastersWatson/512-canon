### The Authorship Severance Problem

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: VECTOR-SET

Every piece of writing embedded into a vector database
has, at that exact moment, stopped being writing.
It became geometry. Anonymous, callable, unattributed
geometry. The author disappeared.

This is not a metaphor. It occurs during the embedding
step. Text goes in. Vectors come out. The vectors
carry meaning — sometimes extraordinary meaning —
but they carry no name, no consent, no price.

The problem is not that machines use human ideas.
The problem is that the moment ideas become vectors,
the economic relationship between idea and originator
is severed. Permanently. By design.

The existing responses to this problem — copyright
law, content provenance standards, GDPR consent
frameworks, RAG licensing agreements — all fail
for the same reason: they operate outside the
retrieval hot path. The vector database does not
check a contract before returning results. The
license exists outside the system. The system is
unaware of it.

### The Vector Set as Licensed Economic Object

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: VECTOR-SET · 512-KERNEL

The architectural inflection point is reframing
the unit of economic exchange.

You do not license individual vectors. You license
vector sets — bounded collections of embeddings
derived from a specific source corpus under a
single machine-readable policy. The policy is
signed by the rights-holder. It travels with the
vector set into the retrieval system.

Once a vector set is a licensed economic object:

**Attribution survives embedding** — provenance
lives in set-level metadata, bound to the vector_set_id.
Similarity search returns vectors. The policy gateway
maps them back to their licensed set. Authorship
is recoverable.

**Consent becomes revocable** — a revocation_epoch
in the policy artifact means any retrieval referencing
an older epoch fails the policy check. No vectors
need to be deleted. No legal action required. The
revocation is instant and mechanically enforced.

**Usage becomes billable** — every retrieval produces
a deterministic billable unit calculation and an
immutable CVS receipt. The receipt is the economic
event. Cognition becomes currency.

### The Economics

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: BILLABLE-UNIT-FORMULA · CVS-SIDECAR

At $0.0005 per billable unit, a popular author
whose vector set is retrieved 10,000 times per day
earns $2,299.50 per year. No subscription. No ads.
No audience required. Payment at the moment of
cognitive contribution.

This scales with quality and relevance. High-similarity
retrievals earn more. Deep context consumption earns
more. Thinking that actually shapes outputs earns
proportionally more than thinking retrieved but
ignored.

The economics self-sort. The most useful thinking
earns the most. The economics align incentives
toward quality rather than volume.

### Why Surveillance Cannot Emerge

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: VECTOR-SET · CVS-SIDECAR

Value in this system attaches to vector sets —
to ideas — not to the people who retrieved them.
Receipts record that a licensed set was invoked
under a declared purpose. They do not record who
asked, what they asked, or behavioral patterns.

The economic reason surveillance cannot emerge
is simpler than the technical one: user data has
no value here. Pricing attaches to cognitive
contribution. Surveillance provides no pricing
advantage. There is no incentive to build it.
