### Intelligence Is Becoming a Commodity

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: 512-KERNEL · EXECUTION-BOUNDARY

AI model capability is commoditizing. Training costs
fall 50% annually. Frontier models that required
hundreds of millions of dollars to train in 2023
will be reproducible for tens of thousands of
dollars by 2027. Capability that is genuinely
scarce today will be table stakes within three
years.

When intelligence commoditizes, the economic value
of AI shifts from the model to the execution layer —
the infrastructure that proves what the model did,
under what constraints, with what outcome. The
intelligence is the input. The execution record
is the asset.

This shift creates the conditions for inference
markets — competitive markets for AI compute where
price is determined by verified delivery, not
asserted capability.

---

### Why Inference Markets Do Not Yet Exist

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: EXECUTION-BOUNDARY · TEMPORAL-INVERSION

Inference markets require three properties that
current AI infrastructure does not provide:

**Proof of delivery** — the buyer must be able to
verify that the declared compute was executed under
declared constraints. Current billing is self-reported
by the vendor. The buyer has no independent proof
of what was actually executed.

**Atomic settlement** — payment and delivery must
settle together. Current billing cycles are monthly.
Autonomous agents executing thousands of inference
requests per second cannot operate on monthly cycles.

**Deterministic pricing** — the cost of a given
request must be computable from observable execution
parameters. Current pricing is opaque — vendors
set prices that buyers cannot independently verify
or challenge.

Without these three properties, inference cannot
be traded in a competitive market. It can only
be purchased from vendors under terms set by
those vendors.

512 and CVS provide all three. Inference markets
become possible the moment execution-time proof
of delivery exists.

### The Market Structure

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: 512-KERNEL · CVS-SIDECAR

A 512-governed inference market is a spot market
for verified compute. The structure:

Buyers declare intent — what compute is requested,
under what constraints, at what maximum price.
The policy gateway evaluates compatibility against
the seller's declared constraints. If compatible,
execution proceeds. CVS records the delivery.
Settlement is atomic — payment and proof settle
together at execution time.

This structure enables competitive dynamics that
do not exist in current AI infrastructure:
- Multiple providers competing on price and performance
- Verifiable service level agreements
- Automated dispute resolution by proof
- Insurance of inference delivery risk
- Composable pipelines with per-step billing

### The Billable Unit

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: BILLABLE-UNIT-FORMULA

Price in an inference market must be deterministic.
The billable unit formula:
```
Billable_Units = depth_multiplier × Σ(similarity_score_i × token_weight_i)
depth_multiplier = 1 + (tokens_consumed ÷ context_budget_declared)
```

The depth multiplier rewards cognitive contribution —
thinking that actually shaped the output earns more
than thinking that was retrieved but ignored. The
formula is auditable by any party with access to
the execution record. No vendor discretion. No
opaque pricing. The cost is provable.

### Intelligence Commoditizes. The Boundary Does Not.

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: 512-KERNEL

The durable economic position in the AI compute
economy belongs to whoever controls the execution
boundary — where liability terminates, compliance
is provable, and contracts are signed.

This mirrors every previous infrastructure transition.
Cloud compute commoditized server hardware and
created AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud as the authority
layer. Mobile commoditized connectivity and created
app stores as the distribution authority layer.

In both cases, economic mass migrated to whoever
controlled the execution boundary. The intelligence
is the commodity. The boundary is the durable asset.

> "Intelligence is upstream. Authority is at the edge.
> Those are not the same place."
> — Jonathan M. Watson
