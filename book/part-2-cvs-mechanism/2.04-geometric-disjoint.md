### The Separation Principle

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: GEOMETRIC-DISJOINT · CVS-SIDECAR

The most important architectural property of CVS
is also the simplest to state: the execution plane
and the evidence plane do not touch.

They run in parallel. They observe the same events.
They never intersect. The evidence plane has no
ability to influence what the execution plane does.
The execution plane has no ability to influence
what the evidence plane records.

This separation — geometric disjoint — is what
makes CVS evidence independent. Independence is
not a property that can be added to an architecture
that lacks it. It must be built in from the beginning
through structural separation.

A witness that can be instructed is not a witness.
An evidence system that can be influenced by the
system it describes is not independent. The geometric
disjoint principle makes instruction and influence
architecturally impossible, not merely prohibited.

---

### Why Separation Cannot Be Partial

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: GEOMETRIC-DISJOINT

Partial separation does not produce partial
independence. It produces no independence.

Consider a CVS implementation where the sidecar
is separate from the execution system in most
respects but shares administrative access. An
administrator with access to both systems can
instruct the execution system to behave differently
and instruct the evidence system to record the
original behavior. The separation is real in nine
of ten dimensions. The tenth dimension — shared
administrative access — destroys independence
completely.

Independence is binary. A witness either has the
ability to be influenced or it does not. There is
no partial independence any more than there is
partial pregnancy.

This is why the conformance requirements for CVS
are absolute. Any configuration that creates a
path — however narrow — by which the execution
system can influence the evidence system violates
geometric disjoint and produces non-conformant
evidence.

### The Camera Analogy Extended

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: GEOMETRIC-DISJOINT · CVS-SIDECAR

The CVS sidecar is a camera mounted above the
factory floor. The analogy extends further than
it first appears.

A camera mounted above a factory floor records
what occurs on the floor. The factory floor does
not know the camera exists. The camera does not
control the factory floor. Neither depends on
the other for its primary function.

Now consider what happens if the factory floor
operator gains access to the camera feed. They
can see what is being recorded. They can use that
knowledge to behave differently when the camera
is watching.

This is the Hawthorne effect — behavior changes
when observed. In most contexts, this is benign.
In an evidence context, it is fatal. Evidence of
behavior that was performed for the camera is
not evidence of normal behavior. It is evidence
of performance.

CVS prevents this through two mechanisms. First,
the sidecar subscribes to event streams the system
already emits for other purposes — monitoring,
debugging, logging. These streams exist regardless
of whether CVS is present. The system cannot
distinguish CVS observation from any other consumer
of its event streams. Second, the sidecar has
no output channel back to the execution system.
It cannot signal that it is recording. It cannot
signal that it has stopped recording. The execution
system has no way to know.

This is geometric disjoint in practice: not just
no influence, but no awareness.

### Prohibited Configurations

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: GEOMETRIC-DISJOINT

The following configurations violate geometric
disjoint and produce non-conformant evidence:

- Shared administrative access between execution
  and evidence systems
- Any feedback channel from evidence plane to
  execution plane
- Evidence systems that can block, pause, or
  modify execution
- Evidence generated inside the system being
  witnessed
- Evidence systems that receive instructions about
  what to record from the execution system
- Key management shared between execution and
  evidence systems
- Evidence systems that participate in constraint
  evaluation

Each of these configurations creates a path by
which the execution system can influence the
evidence record. Each destroys independence.
Each produces evidence that cannot survive
adversarial scrutiny.
