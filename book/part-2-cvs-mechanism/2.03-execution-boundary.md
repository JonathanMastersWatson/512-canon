### Where CVS Operates

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: EXECUTION-BOUNDARY · CVS-SIDECAR

CVS operates at the execution boundary — the precise
moment at which a proposed action becomes an irreversible
state change.

This is not a metaphor. It is a precise architectural
position. The execution boundary is the location in
a system's execution path where proposals cross into
consequences. Before it, actions are hypothetical.
After it, they are facts.

CVS observes this boundary from alongside it. Not
inside it. Not before it. Not after it. Alongside it —
at the exact moment the transition occurs.

This positioning is what makes CVS evidence execution-
time evidence rather than post-hoc reconstruction.
The evidence is generated when the event occurs, not
assembled afterward from artifacts the event left behind.

---

### The Three Observation Points

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: CVS-SIDECAR · EVIDENCE-OBJECT

CVS emits exactly three event types at the execution
boundary. No more. No fewer.

**Pre-Validation**
Captured before constraint evaluation begins.
Records the intent: what action was proposed,
by whom, under what declared purpose, at what
moment. This is the entry record — proof that
a specific proposal entered the evaluation process
at a specific time.

**Validation Result**
Captured after constraint evaluation completes.
Records the outcome of the evaluation: which
constraints were active, how each invariant was
evaluated, and whether the result was allow or deny.
This is the authorization record — proof of what
decision was made and under what rules.

**Post-Execution**
Captured after execution completes or is refused.
Records the actual outcome: did the action execute,
what state changed, or — in the case of refusal —
that no state change occurred. This is the outcome
record — proof of what actually happened.

These three records together constitute a complete
Evidence Object. Remove any one and the object is
incomplete. An incomplete object is not an Evidence
Object. It is a log entry.

---

### Why Three Points and Not One

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: CVS-SIDECAR · EVIDENCE-OBJECT

A single capture point — recording only what happened —
would be simpler. It would also be insufficient.

A record of what happened without a record of what
was authorized produces an execution log. Useful for
debugging. Insufficient for disputes.

A record of what was authorized without a record of
what happened produces an intent log. Useful for
compliance reporting. Insufficient for accountability.

The three-point structure — intent, authorization,
outcome — produces a complete chain from proposal to
consequence. Every link in the chain is independently
verifiable. No link can be altered without breaking
its connection to the others.

This completeness is what makes CVS evidence usable
in adversarial contexts — legal discovery, regulatory
examination, insurance claims — where the counterparty
is actively looking for gaps.

---

### Refusal at the Boundary

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: REFUSAL-PRIMITIVE · EXECUTION-BOUNDARY

When the validation result is deny, CVS records the
refusal as a complete Evidence Object. The refusal
record contains all three observation points:

- Pre-Validation: what was proposed
- Validation Result: which constraint was not satisfied
- Post-Execution: that no state change occurred

A refusal is not a failure. It is a correct outcome.
The constraint operated as designed. The proposed
action did not satisfy the declared rules. The system
worked.

The refusal record is evidence that the constraint
boundary is functioning. A system that produces
refusal records is demonstrating that its governance
is real — not performative. Insurers, regulators,
and counterparties can verify that the boundary
refuses non-compliant proposals, not just that it
claims to.

A pattern of refusals is itself significant evidence.
An agent repeatedly proposing actions that fail
constraint evaluation produces a detectable behavioral
signature in the CVS record before any human review
occurs.
