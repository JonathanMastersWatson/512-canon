### Architecture Follows From Independence

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: CVS-SIDECAR · GEOMETRIC-DISJOINT

The CVS architecture is not a design choice. It is
a logical consequence of the independence requirement.

For CVS evidence to be independently verifiable, the
system producing that evidence must have had no ability
to influence the events it recorded. This single
requirement dictates the entire architecture.

A system that can influence execution cannot produce
independent evidence of execution. A system that
shares authority with the execution plane cannot
produce evidence that is independent of that authority.
A system that interprets evidence as it captures it
cannot produce evidence that is free from interpretation.

Three planes follow from three requirements:
- The execution plane must be independent of evidence capture
- The evidence plane must be independent of execution
- The interpretation plane must be independent of both

Three planes. No trust between them. This is the
architecture.

---

### The Three Planes

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: GEOMETRIC-DISJOINT · CVS-SIDECAR

| Plane | Role | Trusts |
|---|---|---|
| Execution Plane | Primary system — performs actions and state changes | Nothing outside itself |
| Evidence Plane (CVS) | Witness — observes and records cryptographic evidence | Cryptographic proof only |
| Interpretation Plane | External — maps evidence to human and regulatory frameworks | Evidence plane output only |

Each plane is strictly isolated. No plane shares
authority, control paths, or decision logic with
any other plane. Each can fail independently without
cascading to the others.

---

### The Execution Plane

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: GEOMETRIC-DISJOINT · EXECUTION-BOUNDARY

The execution plane is the primary system. It is
whatever system is being witnessed — a payment
processor, an AI agent pipeline, a trading system,
a healthcare records system, a cloud infrastructure
orchestrator.

The execution plane does not know CVS exists. It
does not emit special events for CVS. It does not
modify its behavior because it is being observed.
CVS subscribes to the event streams the execution
plane already generates — the same streams used
for monitoring, debugging, and operational logging.

This is the camera above the factory floor. The
factory floor does not perform differently because
the camera is there. The camera observes what
actually happens, not what the factory floor
chooses to show.

### The Evidence Plane

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: CVS-SIDECAR · EVIDENCE-OBJECT

The evidence plane is CVS. It observes execution
events, time-orders them, cryptographically chains
them, and anchors batches to the public settlement
ledger.

The evidence plane has exactly three capabilities:
- Observe: receive event streams from the execution plane
- Record: produce Evidence Objects from observed events
- Anchor: publish Merkle roots to the settlement ledger

It has exactly zero capabilities that touch execution:
- Cannot block execution
- Cannot modify execution
- Cannot approve or deny actions
- Cannot instruct the execution plane
- Cannot alter Evidence Objects after creation

The moment the evidence plane acquires any capability
that touches execution, it is no longer a witness.
It is a participant. Its evidence is no longer
independent. A participant cannot witness itself.

---

### The Interpretation Plane

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: DISCLOSURE-KERNEL · CVS-SIDECAR

The interpretation plane is external to both
execution and evidence. It maps Evidence Objects
to human frameworks — regulatory requirements,
legal standards, insurance underwriting criteria,
audit specifications.

The interpretation plane never produces primary
evidence. It never captures events. It never
anchors records. It reads the output of the evidence
plane and applies meaning to it.

This separation is critical because interpretation
is where bias enters. A system that interprets
evidence as it captures it cannot produce evidence
that is free from that interpretation. A court
that wrote its own records would not be trusted.
A witness that annotated their observations with
conclusions would not be credible. The same principle
applies here.

Interpretation happens downstream. Always. Never
at capture.

### Why No Trust Between Planes

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: GEOMETRIC-DISJOINT · CVS-SIDECAR

The no-trust model is not paranoia. It is the only
model that produces independently verifiable evidence.

If the evidence plane trusted the execution plane,
the execution plane could instruct the evidence
plane to record things differently. Evidence would
reflect what the execution plane wanted recorded,
not what occurred.

If the execution plane trusted the evidence plane,
a compromised evidence plane could influence
execution decisions. The evidence plane would cease
to be a passive observer.

If the interpretation plane trusted either, its
interpretations would be contaminated by the
interests of the systems producing the evidence
it interprets.

Trust between planes is the mechanism by which
independence is destroyed. No trust between planes
is the mechanism by which independence is preserved.
