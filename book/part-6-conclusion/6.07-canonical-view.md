### The Argument in Full

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: 512-KERNEL · EXECUTION-BOUNDARY

This book has made one argument from first principles
to civilisational consequence. It is stated here
in full, in sequence, without elaboration.

**The physics:**
The speed of light bounds coordination speed.
Distributed execution at machine speed cannot
wait for centralized governance. Physics forces
governance to the execution boundary.

**The boundary:**
Every execution system has a commit point — the
moment at which a proposed action becomes an
irreversible state change, the bit-flip. Before it, proposals.
After it, facts. Authority that does not operate
at the commit point does not govern execution.
It observes it.

**The constraint:**
The minimum conditions that any legitimate voluntary
interaction at machine speed must satisfy are seven.
Not six. Not eight. Seven. They are stated in 466
bytes. They are immutable. Adherence is binary.

**The proof:**
512 defines what must be true at the execution
boundary. CVS proves it was true. A system cannot
be the authoritative witness to its own failure.
CVS is the independent witness. Receipts not logs.
Evidence not explanation.

**The economics:**
$50 trillion in friction exists because legitimacy
must be reconstructed rather than verified.
When legitimacy moves to the execution boundary,
that friction disappears. Not decreases. Disappears.

**The institutions:**
Courts, regulators, and governments do not lose
their authority. They lose the mechanisms by which
illegitimate authority has sustained itself —
opacity, delay, and unilateral discretion. Authority
that depends on legitimacy rather than coercion
gains infrastructure. Authority that depends on
coercion loses cover.

**The philosophy:**
Natural rights endure not because they are poetic
but because they scale under distributed execution.
The seven invariants are not moral preferences.
They are physical necessities that correspond to
moral conclusions because both describe the same
structural properties of voluntary interaction.

**The inevitability:**
Physics forces this. The resistance will follow
the Five Responses of Power. The responses will
slow adoption. They will not prevent it. The cost
of not having execution-time legitimacy at machine
speed is compounding every year that adoption is
delayed. The inevitable is not a prediction.
It is a description of what happens when friction
compounds in one direction and relief compounds
in the other.

### From c to Ubiquity

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: 512-KERNEL

The title of this book/manuscript is the path of the argument.

c — the speed of light — is the physical constant
that bounds coordination speed and forces execution
to the edge. It is the starting point that makes
everything else necessary.

Ubiquity — the condition that emerges when execution-time
constraint verification is embedded everywhere —
is the destination. Not mandated. Not promoted.
Adopted because the cost of operating without it
exceeds the cost of implementing it.

HTTPS did not become ubiquitous because someone
decided it should. It became ubiquitous because
every browser, payment processor, and enterprise
procurement policy made operating without it
progressively more expensive than operating with it.

512 follows the same path. Not from advocacy.
From physics, economics, and the accumulated cost
of legitimacy uncertainty at civilisational scale.

The path from c to ubiquity is the path this
book has mapped. It is not complete. The destination
has not been reached. The direction is determined.
