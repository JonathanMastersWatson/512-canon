### The Canonical Statement

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: CVS-SIDECAR

The Cryptographic Verification Sidecar is an
independent witness architecture for execution-time
evidence.

It sits alongside a running system — never inside it.
It records what occurred, when it occurred, and
under what declared constraints.
It does not block execution.
It does not interpret intent.
It does not decide outcomes.
It does not enforce policy.

It witnesses.

That is its complete function. A sidecar that does
anything more than witness is not CVS. Its evidence
is not independent. Its records are arguments,
not proofs.

512 defines what must be true at the execution
boundary. CVS proves it was true.

512 is the constraint. CVS is the proof.

### The Five Properties — Restated

Source: Jonathan M. Watson | 512 / CVS — Watson
Concept-ID: CVS-SIDECAR

A CVS-conforming implementation satisfies five
non-negotiable properties. They are restated here
as the canonical reference:

**Fail-Open**
Evidence systems must never block, delay, or
interrupt execution. If CVS is offline, execution
continues. A visible gap is acceptable.
An unobservable failure is a defect.

**Witness-Only**
The sidecar may observe and record. It may never
act, decide, or enforce. The moment CVS participates
in decisions, its independence is compromised
and its evidence becomes an argument rather than
a record.

**Authority Limits**
No hidden, delegated, or implicit power is permitted.
CVS has no control surface. It cannot be used to
govern. It can only be used to prove.

**Selective Disclosure**
Transparency must be precise, bounded, and minimal.
CVS records that an event occurred — not who the
end user was, what they asked, or how they behave
over time. The Disclosure Kernel enables scoped
proof without full exposure.

**Independent Verification**
Verification must be possible without trusting
the operator. Any third party must be able to
verify evidence independently without privileged
system access. The settlement layer anchor makes
this possible.
